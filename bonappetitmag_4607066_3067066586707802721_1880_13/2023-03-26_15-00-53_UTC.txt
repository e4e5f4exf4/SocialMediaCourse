“Lay them out in the sun on some old screen doors; they can touch each other. Don’t overthink it.” That’s what @lisamariedonovan’s aunt told her over a decade ago about the best way to dry apples. “You can also do it in the back of your station wagon, if you got one,” she continued. “The sun hits a car window just right during harvest time.” Donavan had neither, and still doesn’t. But she keeps coming back to this apple recipe to honor her long lineage of makers. Read her essay and others about Appalachian food at the link in bio.
